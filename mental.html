Mental Health Screening

Home / Mental Health Screening

Self-Assessment Tools

Our confidential mental health screenings can help you identify potential concerns and determine if you might benefit from professional support. These tools are not diagnostic, but they can provide valuable insight into your mental wellbeing.

Important: These screenings are not a substitute for professional diagnosis or treatment. If you're in crisis or experiencing thoughts of suicide, please call our crisis line at 1-800-273-TALK (8255) or text HOME to 741741 immediately.

Select a screening to begin:

Choose the assessment that best matches your current concerns. Each screening takes about 3-5 minutes to complete.

Depression

Assesses mood, interest in activities, energy levels, and other symptoms of depression.

Anxiety

Evaluates feelings of nervousness, worry, and physical symptoms of anxiety.

PTSD

Screens for symptoms related to traumatic experiences.

Bipolar Disorder

Assesses for symptoms of both depression and mania/hypomania.

Alcohol Use

Evaluates patterns of alcohol consumption and related concerns.

ADHD

Screens for symptoms of attention deficit hyperactivity disorder.

Depression Screening

Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by any of the following problems?

1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things

2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless

3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much

4. Feeling tired or having little energy

5. Poor appetite or overeating

6. Feeling bad about yourself - or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down

7. Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television

8. Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed. Or the opposite - being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual

9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead or of hurting yourself in some way

Your Depression Screening Results

Recommendations

Consult a professional: Based on your results, we recommend speaking with a mental health professional for further evaluation. They can provide an accurate diagnosis and discuss treatment options with you.

Educational resources: Learn more about depression, its symptoms, and treatment options through our curated resources.

Support groups: Consider joining a support group where you can connect with others who understand what you're experiencing.

Self-care strategies: Regular exercise, healthy eating, and good sleep habits can all help manage symptoms of depression.
